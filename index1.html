Maleficent (2014)

A Bold Retelling of a Timeless Fairytale

About the Film

Director: Robert Stromberg Starring: Angelina Jolie, Elle Fanning, Sharlto Copley Genre: Fantasy, Drama, Adventure

Maleficent reimagines the iconic Disney villain from Sleeping Beauty as a complex and tragic antihero. The story dives deep into themes of betrayal, revenge, maternal love, and redemption in a dark, visually mesmerizing world.

Story Summary

In a mystical land called the Moors, Maleficent is a powerful fairy who serves as protector of her realm. She falls in love with a human named Stefan. As time passes, Stefan betrays her in a cruel act of ambition, cutting off her wings to become king. Enraged and heartbroken, Maleficent curses his daughter, Aurora, to fall into a death-like sleep on her 16th birthday.

But as Aurora grows, Maleficent finds herself drawn to the girl, forming an unexpected bond. The curse she cast becomes the pain she regrets, and when Aurora falls, it is Maleficent's own kiss of maternal love that awakens her — redefining what “true love” means.

Key Themes

  • Betrayal & Pain: The emotional roots of Maleficent’s transformation
  • Power & Revenge: How power can be corrupted when born of grief
  • Redefining True Love: Exploring maternal love as the purest form
  • Healing & Forgiveness: The slow path back from darkness
